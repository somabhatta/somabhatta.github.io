About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Institute for Gravitational and Subatomic Physics (GRASP) at Utrecht University, working with Prof. dr. R.J.M. Snellings. I recently completed my Ph.D. at Stony Brook University under the supervision of Prof. Jiangyong Jia.

My research focuses on probing the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), a primordial state of matter that existed microseconds after the Big Bang. Understanding the properties of this QCD matter is essential for constraining its critical point and explaining its phase transition into hadronic matter in the universe. This involves studying key characteristics of QGP such as its viscosities, the speed of sound in the medium, and the origin of collectivity in heavy-ion collisions.

In recent years, my research has made substantial contributions toward addressing long-standing issues using heavy-ion collision data collected with the ATLAS detector at the LHC and the STAR detector at RHIC.

Research Highlights

Imaging Shapes of Atomic Nuclei in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions
STAR Collaboration, Nature 635, 67–72 (2024) 94 citations
Impact: This landmark study pioneered a novel paradigm for nuclear structure physics, demonstrating for the first time that relativistic heavy-ion collisions can precisely image the three-dimensional shape of atomic nuclei. This work bridged the gap between high-energy and low-energy nuclear physics and resolved a major uncertainty that had long constrained the precision of Quark-Gluon Plasma studies.
Evidence for the collective nature of radial flow in Pb+Pb collisions
ATLAS Collaboration, Accepted in PRL (Editor's suggestion), arXiv:2503.24125 15 citations
Impact: This analysis established an entirely new observational window into QGP dynamics. By performing the first measurement of the transverse momentum dependence of radial flow fluctuations, I demonstrated that radial flow is a collective phenomenon. This work establishes v₀(pT) as a powerful new tool with high sensitivity to the QGP's bulk viscosity.
Disentangling sources of momentum fluctuations in Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb collisions
ATLAS Collaboration, PRL 133, 252301 (2024) 29 citations
Impact: This work achieved a critical breakthrough by experimentally isolating distinct sources of initial-state fluctuations for the first time. By disentangling geometric effects from intrinsic quantum fluctuations, my analysis solved a long-standing modeling challenge and enabled a cleaner extraction of QGP properties.
