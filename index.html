About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Institute for Gravitational and Subatomic Physics (GRASP) at Utrecht University, working with Prof. dr. R.J.M. Snellings. I am also affiliated with NIKHEF and actively work within the ALICE Collaboration at CERN and the STAR Collaboration at RHIC.

I recently completed my Ph.D. at Stony Brook University under the supervision of Prof. Jiangyong Jia. My research focuses on probing the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), a primordial state of matter that existed microseconds after the Big Bang. I study its key characteristics, such as viscosity, speed of sound, and the origin of collectivity in heavy-ion collisions.

Full Publication List

A.i) Selected First/Primary Author Experimental Papers

Imaging Shapes of Atomic Nuclei in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions
STAR Collaboration, Nature 635, 67–72 (2024)
Journal 94 Citations
Scientific Impact: This landmark study pioneered a novel paradigm for nuclear structure physics, demonstrating for the first time that relativistic heavy-ion collisions can precisely image the three-dimensional shape of atomic nuclei. This work bridged the gap between high-energy and low-energy nuclear physics and resolved a major uncertainty that had long constrained the precision of Quark-Gluon Plasma studies.
Evidence for the collective nature of radial flow in Pb+Pb collisions with the ATLAS detector
ATLAS Collaboration, Accepted in PRL (Editor's Suggestion), arXiv:2503.24125
arXiv 15 Citations
Scientific Impact: This analysis established an entirely new observational window into QGP dynamics. By performing the first measurement of the transverse momentum dependence of radial flow fluctuations, I demonstrated that radial flow is a collective phenomenon. This work establishes v0(pT) as a powerful new tool with high sensitivity to the QGP's bulk viscosity. This was the first measurement to demonstrate that radial flow fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions also behave collectively.
Disentangling sources of momentum fluctuations in Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb collisions with the ATLAS detector
ATLAS Collaboration, PRL 133, 252301 (2024)
Journal 29 Citations
Scientific Impact: This work achieved a critical breakthrough by experimentally isolating distinct sources of initial-state fluctuations for the first time. By disentangling geometric effects from intrinsic quantum fluctuations, my analysis solved a long-standing modeling challenge.
Correlations between flow and transverse momentum in Xe+Xe and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC with the ATLAS detector
ATLAS Collaboration, PRC 105, 054910 (2023)
Journal 75 Citations
Scientific Impact: This publication laid the essential groundwork for the subsequent landmark Nature paper on nuclear imaging. It systematically established the vn-[pT] correlation as a precision tool for probing initial-state geometry.
