About Me

Postdoctoral Researcher at the Institute for Gravitational and Subatomic Physics (GRASP), Utrecht University.

Working with Prof. dr. R.J.M. Snellings.

I recently completed my Ph.D. at Stony Brook University under the supervision of Prof. Jiangyong Jia. My research focuses on probing the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), a primordial state of matter that existed microseconds after the Big Bang.

Research Highlights

Imaging Shapes of Atomic Nuclei

STAR Collaboration, Nature 635, 67–72 (2024)

94 citations

Impact: Pioneered a novel paradigm for nuclear structure physics, demonstrating that relativistic heavy-ion collisions can precisely image the three-dimensional shape of atomic nuclei.

Collective nature of radial flow in Pb+Pb

ATLAS Collaboration, Accepted in PRL

15 citations

Impact: Established an entirely new observational window into QGP dynamics. Demonstrated radial flow is a collective phenomenon and established v₀(pT) as a tool for bulk viscosity.

Disentangling sources of momentum fluctuations

ATLAS Collaboration, PRL 133, 252301 (2024)

29 citations

Impact: Achieved a critical breakthrough by experimentally isolating distinct sources of initial-state fluctuations for the first time.
