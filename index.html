About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher affiliated with both the Institute for Gravitational and Subatomic Physics (GRASP) at Utrecht University and NIKHEF. I work with Prof. dr. R.J.M. Snellings within the ALICE Collaboration at CERN.

Previously, I completed my Ph.D. at Stony Brook University under the supervision of Prof. Jiangyong Jia. My research probes the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), focusing on viscosities, the speed of sound in the medium, and the origin of collectivity in heavy-ion collisions using data from ATLAS (LHC) and STAR (RHIC).

Impact: Established v₀(pT) as a tool sensitive to QGP bulk viscosity and demonstrated the collective nature of radial flow.

Impact: Experimentally isolated distinct sources of initial-state fluctuations, solving a long-standing modeling challenge.
